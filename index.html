DiffMR: Training-Free Diffusion Spherical Linear Interpolation Guidance for Music Restoration

Anonymous Authors

Abstract

Music restoration encompasses a range of tasks such as inpainting, super-resolution, dereverberation, and phase retrieval, all of which aim to recover high-quality audio from degraded inputs. While recent diffusion models have shown promise for generative audio modeling, their application to restoration tasks typically requires task-specific training or fine-tuning. Moreover, existing inference time optimization methods often produce noisy sample updates that deviate from the modelâ€™s prior distribution, potentially compromising generation quality. To address these problems, we propose DiffMR, a training-free framework for music restoration based on pretrained diffusion models. Our method introduces Diffusion Spherical Linear Interpolation Guidance (DSLIG), a geometry-aware guidance strategy that incorporates task-specific loss gradients during inference while ensuring that noisy sample updates remain on the original Spherical Gaussian. By interpreting the diffusion space as a high-dimensional sphere, DSLIG steers the denoising trajectory along geodesics toward perceptually optimal regions without deviating from the prior distribution. We evaluate DiffMR across four music restoration tasks and demonstrate that it achieves superior performance compared to existing methods, all in a fully zero-shot setting without any additional training.
